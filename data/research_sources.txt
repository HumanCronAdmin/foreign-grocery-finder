== GAPS / ITEMS NEEDING MORE VERIFICATION ==

Items where evidence is thin or unverified:
- Black beans: Only Seijo Ishii "sometimes" has them. Hard to find generally
- Lentils: Hard to find at chain stores. Best sources are Indian grocery stores or Amazon
- Quinoa: Confirmed at Amazon but limited physical store verification
- Baking soda: Available but Japanese label (重曹) makes it findable at regular stores too
- Vanilla extract: Pure extract is rare; Japan mostly sells vanilla essence
- Chili flakes: Kaldi likely has them but specific verification is thin
- Paprika: Kaldi has it (in harissa at minimum), standalone needs verification

Stores with least data:
- Don Quijote: General confirmation of imported food section with peanut butter, tahini, hot sauce, muesli/granola, salsa. But specific item-level data is weak
- Seijo Ishii: Known for cheese and imported goods but specific product listings are sparse in public sources

== NOTES FOR DATA FILE ==

1. Prices are approximate and may vary by location/date
2. Gyomu Super has most verified data and best prices for imported basics
3. Kaldi strongest for spices, sauces, and specialty international items
4. Costco best for bulk cheese, oats, peanut butter (requires membership ¥4,840/year)
5. Seijo Ishii premium pricing but widest cheese selection
6. Don Quijote carries some items but inconsistent across locations
7. Amazon Japan is the reliable fallback for everything but at higher prices
8. Product availability varies by store location — larger/urban stores carry more
