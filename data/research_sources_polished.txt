== Blank period/items requiring verification

Items with little or no evidence or verification
- Black beans: Only Seijo Ishii has them "occasionally". Generally difficult to obtain.
- Lentils: Hard to find in chain stores. Indian grocery stores or Amazon are best.
- Quinoa: verified on Amazon, but limited availability in actual stores.
- Baking soda: available, but due to the Japanese label (baking soda), also available in general stores.
- Vanilla Essence: Pure essence is rare.
- Chili flakes: likely to be found in Cardi, but concrete verification is thin.
- Paprika: Available in Cardi (at least in harissa).

Stores with the least data
- Don Quijote: Don Quijote: roughly verified imported food department with peanut butter, tahini, hot sauce, muesli/granola, and salsa. However, specific item-level data is weak
- Seijo Ishii: known for cheese and imported products, but specific product listings are sparse in public documents

== Notes on data files

1. Prices are approximate and may vary by location/date.
2. Gyoem Supermarket has the most verified data and is the best price for imported basics.
3. Cardi is the best for spices, sauces, and foreign specialty products.
4. Costco is best for bulk purchases of cheese, oats, and peanut butter.
Seijo Ishii: Premium prices, but the widest selection of cheese.
Don Quijote carries some products, but the selection varies from store to store.
Amazon Japan is reliable but expensive.
8. Different stores have different assortments.
